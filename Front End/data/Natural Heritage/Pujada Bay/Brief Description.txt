Pujada Bay is a majestic cove characterized by its turquoise waters, powdery white-sand beaches, and a dramatic backdrop of mountain ranges. It is located downstream of the Mount Hamiguitan Range Wildlife Sanctuary, a UNESCO World Heritage site, and serves as a vital ecological link between the highlands and the ocean (H2O). The bay features a complex network of fertile coral reefs (1,175.75 ha), expansive seagrass beds (610.44 ha), and lush mangrove forests (262.71 ha).
The bay's geography is punctuated by alluring islands and islets, most notably Sleeping Dinosaur, Pujada, Waniban, Oak, and Ivy. It encompasses ten coastal communities—Macambol, Mamali, Dawan, Badas, Central, Matiao, Dahican, Bobon, Tamisan, and Lawigan—where residents interact with nature for both recreation and sustainable livelihoods.
