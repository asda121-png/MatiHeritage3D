The Mamacao Tree (Dracontomelon dao), stands as a living landmark in Purok Mamacaw, Barangay Bobon. Believed to have been planted during the American Colonial Period, the tree features a massive trunk  that has grown deep-rooted alongside the community that now bears its name. The site holds deep historical and spiritual significance: during World War II, it served as a place of refuge known as Tagunsaang, while local lore tells of a sacred bird that brought the tree to this spot, where it remains protected by spirits to this day. Though it has faced modern challenges such as infrastructure development and the harvesting of its sturdy roots for agricultural tools, the Mamacaw remains in excellent condition, serving as a resilient symbol of the heritage and natural beauty of the City of Mati.
