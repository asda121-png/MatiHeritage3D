The Tay-Tay Daga, popularly known as the Sleeping Dinosaur, is a majestic natural landmark situated in Sitio Baso, Barangay Badas, City of Mati. Spanning approximately 579 hectares, this mountain is famous for its rare and unusual physical formation that naturally resembles a dinosaur resting upon the waters of Pujada Bay. Beyond its aesthetic allure, the site holds profound historical and spiritual significance: it served as a Japanese docking point during World War II, has long been home to indigenous communities, and is steeped in Mandaya language ‘Yag Tay-Tay Yang Daga Sang Butay,’ involving guardian spirits and the legendary ‘white lady’ of the highlands whom the mountain is named. Formally recognized under Resolution No. 61, Series of 2019, the Sleeping Dinosaur stands as a protected heritage landscape, symbolizing the rich cultural identity and natural grandeur of Mati.
