Mati City history

Timeline of Mati City

From a Mandaya settlement on the east coast to the capital of Davao Oriental — key milestones that shaped the city we document today.

01
1861 Origins

Settlement founded

Captain Prudencio Garcia and Juan Nazareno established Mati on the east coast. The name comes from the Mandaya word Maa-ti — a creek said to dry quickly even after heavy rain.

02
1903 Municipality

Municipality established

On October 29, Mati became a regular municipality under Act No. 21, joining other east-coast towns in a formal local government system.

03
1967 Capital

Provincial capital

Mati was designated capital of Davao Oriental, cementing its role as the province’s political and economic center on the Philippine Sea coast.

04
2003 Centennial

100 years as a municipality

Mati marked its centennial — a century since Act No. 21 — celebrating its growth from a coastal settlement into a thriving provincial hub.

05
2007 Cityhood

Republic Act No. 9408

The municipality was converted into a component city. Voters ratified the law in June, beginning Mati’s path toward full city status.

06
2011 Today

Cityhood affirmed

The Supreme Court upheld Mati’s cityhood on February 15. Mati City — the only city in Davao Oriental — celebrates its foundation day every June 19.
